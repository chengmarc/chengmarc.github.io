The Afterimage

You live inside a body you never built,
moving along a path you never chose,
guided by a map that redraws itself
every time a memory trembles.

Your thoughts arrive pre-written.
Your emotions echo from older rooms.
What you call “I”
is the shadow of something already in motion—
the afterimage of a mind trying to catch itself.

You are carried by your past
more than you carry it.
Old versions of you linger in the corridors,
leaving fingerprints on every decision,
whispering their preferences,
their fears,
their unfinished sentences.

A self is not a fortress.
It’s a sandcastle that believes in its walls,
rewritten each morning,
reworn each night,
reshaped by every person who ever mattered.

But there is a quiet center—
a small hum of presence—
the moment-to-moment fact of existing.
The one thing not inherited,
not remembered,
not predicted.

Just happening.

You are that moment.
And also the ghosts around it.
And also the hands that keep rebuilding the shape.

A self is not one thing.
It is the place where many things gather
and pretend to be one.

And somehow,
that illusion is enough
to keep the ship moving forward.
